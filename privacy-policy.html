2. Personal Data We Collect

3. How We Collect Your Data

  • Online: You submit your name & email via our website contact form.
  • Telephone: You may provide your name & email when you call us.

4. Purposes of Processing & Legal Bases

  • Responding to enquiries
    Purpose: To reply to your message and discuss potential projects.
    Legal basis: Necessary for taking steps at your request prior to entering a contract (UK GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Consultancy/project delivery
    Purpose: Performance of our contract with you.
    Legal basis: Contractual necessity (UK GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)).

We do not send marketing emails or newsletters at this time.

5. Cookies & Tracking

  • We use Google Analytics to gain insights into website usage.
  • You’ll see a cookie-consent banner on first visit; you can opt out or withdraw consent at any time via the banner controls.

6. Data Sharing

  • Contact-form data is not shared with any third-party marketing or CRM tools.
  • We store data in our Microsoft Azure tenancy under UK/EU data-residency settings.
  • Any personal data accessed for a client project is processed under their security procedures and not retained by us after contract completion.

7. International Transfers

We do not transfer personal data outside the UK or EEA.

8. Data Retention

  • Contact-form submissions & leads: Retained for up to 12 months, then purged.
  • Client project data: No personal data retained after contract ends.
  • Non-personal project documents: May be retained longer for related future work.

9. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Rectify inaccurate data
  • Erase your data (“right to be forgotten”)
  • Restrict processing
  • Object to processing
  • Data portability

To exercise any right or submit a Subject Access Request (SAR), contact our DPO at hello@iternova.co.uk. We will respond within one month, per ICO guidelines.

10. Security Measures

  • Encryption at rest (Azure) and in transit (TLS).
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for external data sharing.
  • Role-based access controls.
  • Regular backups and disaster-recovery procedures.
  • Mandatory GDPR & data-security training for all staff.

11. Children

Our services and website are aimed at businesses. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 16.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. The “Last updated” date above will change accordingly. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
